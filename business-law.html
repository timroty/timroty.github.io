Digital Laws and Their Consequences for Freedom of Expression
Introduction
The legal tradition in China is founded on an ethical basis completely foreign to what many in the West are acquainted with. The ethics that underpin law in China is as varied as the amalgamation of influences that now define the character of the country itself. The Chinese legal code in contemporary use can find traces of its origin in the ancient bureaucracies employed by classical Chinese emperors, various religious teachings, modified german civil law, and even soviet law subsequent to the rise of communism (https://mckinneylaw.iu.edu/iiclr/pdf/vol6p327.pdf). However, throughout the tumult that has defined the evolution of the Chinese legal code, one constant has been the influence of confucianism and legalism (hastings).
